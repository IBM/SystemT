<span class="body-text-small">The homepage is designed to further advance the foundation of profiled delivery of content, as well as new tools and applications to help users manage their workflow, access and share information, collaborate, leverage the organization's knowledge base and create a more open culture. A great deal more content is available that users can access directly from their homepage which is divided into mini-pages called "portlets."</span>

<a name=what>
<span class="head-small">What is a portlet</span><br /><br />
<span class="body-text-small">Portlets are customized content areas designed to deliver personalized information to users based on their profile. (To experience all that each portlet has to offer you must create your w3 profile. Go to <a href="/eworkplace/profile/settings">Profile Settings</a>, scroll down to Personal Information and click on "Modify." Next, select your preferences. When you are finished, hit "Submit." Your w3 homepage will be tailored to your particular needs and interests.)
</span>

<p class="body-text-small">Each homepage portlet is based on a particular type of activity (e.g., reading the news, or carrying out HR-related processes, or finding job-related information) and offers added functionality &#151; some more than others. For example, most portlets can be minimized (its text and graphics are hidden) and maximized (expanded to a full screen view). All users, regardless of whether they have created a w3 profile, will see information in each portlet. Profiled users, however, will experience significantly richer &#151; and more &#151; content based on their profile settings.
</p>

<a name=tech>
<span class="head-small">What is the technology behind the new homepage</span><br /><br />
<span class="body-text-small">The homepage is built on IBM's WebSphere, which uses a combination of Java Server Pages (.jsp) and servlets to deliver new functionality. The infrastructure is designed to use traditional API's, as well as Web services-based methods, which will increase over time as new services are published to the w3 Universal Description, Discovery and Integration (UDDI) server. Under UDDI, all business units will be able to register in the intranet-based services directory to make their offerings widely available. Since IBM provides technical and business services through industry standards like SOAP and XML over HTTP, such availability will mean higher reuse and lower expense-to-revenue ratios. The portlets' mini Web-based applications are treated as independent components and can be developed, deployed and maintained as separate entities. With e-Workplace, the w3 team is establishing a base application platform that will allow added functionality through new portlets, without the need to redesign the site for every addition or update.</span>
