<a name=edit>
<p class="body-text-small"><span class="art-summary"><b>'Edit' Icon</b></span><br />
When you click on the edit icon in the title bar of a portlet, you will be taken to a page where you can edit the content for that portlet.</p>
<a name=help>
<p class="body-text-small"><span class="art-summary"><b>'Help' Icon</b></span><br />By clicking on the question mark in the title bar of a portlet, you will be taken to its corresponding Help page.</p>
<a name=minimize>
<p class="body-text-small"><span class="art-summary"><b>'Minimize' Icon</b></span><br />This lets you minimize the a portlet on the homepage. Click on it to collapse the content of the section. It will remove the portlet's text and graphics. All you will see is the portlet's title bar, which will include a restore icon of two overlapping screens.</p>

<a name=restore>
<p class="body-text-small"><span class="art-summary"><b>'Restore' Icon</b></span><br />An icon of two screens in the title bar of a portlet indicates that the portlet is currently minimized (its text and graphics are hidden) or maximized (full screen). To restore the portlet to its default view, click on the restore icon.</p>
<a name=maximize>
<span class="body-text-small"><span class="art-summary"><b>'Maximize' Icon</b></span><br />This lets you maximize the portlet on the homepage. Click on it to expand the section to fill the screen. In its maximized view, the portlet's title bar will include a restore icon of two overlapping screens.</span>

<a name=tech>
<span class="head-small">What is the technology behind the new homepage</span><br /><br />
<span class="body-text-small">The homepage is built on IBM's WebSphere, which uses a combination of Java Server Pages (.jsp) and servlets to deliver new functionality. The infrastructure is designed to use traditional API's, as well as Web services-based methods, which will increase over time as new services are published to the w3 Universal Description, Discovery and Integration (UDDI) server. Under UDDI, all business units will be able to register in the intranet-based services directory to make their offerings widely available. Since IBM provides technical and business services through industry standards like SOAP and XML over HTTP, such availability will mean higher reuse and lower expense-to-revenue ratios. The portlets' mini Web-based applications are treated as independent components and can be developed, deployed and maintained as separate entities. With e-Workplace, the w3 team is establishing a base application platform that will allow added functionality through new portlets, without the need to redesign the site for every addition or update.</span>
